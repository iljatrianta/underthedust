The safety nets still proved insufficient!

They got off to a slow start and were too limited, leaving South Limburg to struggle for years with high unemployment, poverty, and economic disadvantage.

Unemployment remained structurally much higher than in the rest of the Netherlands. In some years, nearly double the national average.

There were unemployment benefits, social assistance, and early retirement schemes. Retraining and job placement were also offered.

but these could not absorb the massive job losses.

Besides the jobs lost due to the mine closures, other companies also went bankrupt or downsized, causing new employment opportunities to disappear.

For many people, the safety nets meant long-term dependency instead of a new start. Unemployment, disability benefits, and early retirement became reality for a large part of the region.

In the 1970s/80s, Operation Hartslag was launched to give South Limburg new prospects after the mine closures.


The government wanted to free the region from its dependence on the mining industry.

Operation Hartslag focused on new companies, jobs, and investments in industry, services, and infrastructure.

Knowledge and education were also used to strengthen the economy structurally. Recovery was slow, but it laid the foundation for South Limburg's economic transformation.
